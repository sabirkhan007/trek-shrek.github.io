Do Dham Tour

₹ 12,999 / Per person

Overview

Location: Rudraprayag, Uttarakhand

Trek Duration: 05 Days

Difficulty Level: Moderate to Difficult

Trekking Distance: 28 Km

Best Time: April to June

Age Limit: 12 to 50 Years

Trek to a place where divinity murmurs in the air! Kedarnath, a place known as an abode of Lord Shiva, captivates you with the glory it holds. It is a famous Hindu pilgrimage destination where thousands of devotees from all over the world come in pursuit to attain ‘Moksha’.

05 Days / 04 Nights
6-15 persons
Trekking distance : 62 Km

Short Itinerary

DAY 1: Haridwar - Phata

DAY 2: Phata - Gaurikund - Kedarnath

DAY 3: Kedarnath - Gopeshwar

DAY 4: Gopeshwar - Badrinath

DAY 5: Joshimath - Haridwar

Detail Itinerary

Day 1: Haridwar - Phata

Explore the Yoga Capital of India - Rishikesh

You will be picked up from Haridwar in the morning and taken to Phata.
En route, witness the beauty of the Yoga capital of India.
The journey will cover Byasi, Devprayag, Srinagar, Rudraprayag, and Agastmuni hill stations along the way.
Stop for lunch midway, and continue your journey to the destination in the evening.
Check-in at the hotel and enjoy your evening.

Overnight stay at Phata.

Day 2: Phata - Gaurikund - Kedarnath

Trail to pristine abode of Lord Shiva

Wake up with the mesmerizing view of mountains all across in Phata.
Before embarking towards Gaurikund, have a delicious breakfast in the morning.
Gauri Kund is a place where Lord Shiva admitted His love for Lord Gauri, and it is also a characteristic pool with warm water known for relieving skin maladies.
Start the trek to Kedarnath from Gauri Kund.
You can take ponies or palanquins for the journey at your own cost.
Trek through the beautiful valley with a river stream flowing in between.
Have a lunch stop in between and continue the journey.
You will reach the Kedarnath Temple, situated right at the center of the valley, surrounded by mountains that seem to provide safety to the temple.
After offering prayers at Kedarnath, have dinner and rest.
Overnight stay and dinner will be at Kedarnath.

Day 3: Kedarnath - Gopeshwar

Trek down to Sonprayag

In the morning, all participants can visit the Kedarnath temple.
Explore the views of the Himalayas, Bhimshila, and the Mandakini river.
Don't forget to capture memories for life with your mates.
After visiting the temple, we will trek back towards Sonprayag.
The night stay and dinner will be at Gopeshwar, which is famous for Gopeshwar Mahadev temple and Rudranath temple (one of Panch Kedar).

Day 4: Gopeshwar - Badrinath

Take the blessings of Lord Vishnu at Badrinath temple

Wake up early and have breakfast, then begin your journey to the holy temple of Badrinath.
On your journey, you will see some of the most mesmerizing views.
Before visiting the temple of Badrinath, you will visit the last village of India, Mana Village.
After sightseeing at Mana Village, you will proceed to the darshan at Badrinath temple.
After completing darshan at Badrinath, you will return to the hotel at Joshimath, the base camp of Auli.
Overnight stay at the hotel.

Day 5: Joshimath - Haridwar

Departure to Haridwar

Refuel yourself with a substantial breakfast at the inn.
After breakfast, depart for Haridwar.
Conclude the trip with glorious memories after reaching Haridwar.
