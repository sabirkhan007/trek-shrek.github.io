Overview

Region: Uttarakhand

Trek Duration: 08 Days

Trek Difficulty: Difficult

Approx Trekking Km: On foot 64 Kms - By taxi 425 kms

Base Camp: Sankri Village

Nearest Bus/Railway Station/Airport: Dehradun

Best Time: May, June, September, October, November

If you wanna discover a place akin to heaven, then Bali Pass Trek (16,200 Ft. Altitude & 60 Kms. Distance) is just the one. The way to Bali Pass goes through some of the best places of Uttarakhand. The very first place you will cross is Yamunotri, one of the Dhams of Char Dham of Uttarakhand. This trek is a rare one and hence is more difficult than the other trek. On the way to it, you will also pass Har ki Doon valley, Ruinsara lake, and Osla village. After reaching you will see the real beauty of nature. You will be able to see amazing vistas of Swargrohini & Bandarpoonch peaks. Apart from these peaks, you will also see many other peaks that look gorgeous and give a soothing effect.

If you are a true adventure lover and want to adventure in rare places with slightly difficult terrain then you must hit this place at least once. The way to Bali Pass Trek encompasses several traditional villages that look beautiful. You will definitely get a feeling like you have traveled the time in the backward direction. In the villages, you will see the traditional lifestyle of people living there. It's truly a place to adore. Soon you will also travel through Debshu Bugyal which makes the Bali Pass Trek even more beautiful. Then comes Ruinsara Tal which is the epitome of beauty and completes this trek. It possesses such crystal clear water in it that you can see reflections of things that are in its proximity.

Now, before reaching the destination you will have to climb a summit that is capable enough to satisfy your adventurous passion. It's a difficult one but meanwhile a satisfactory one. You willsee it covered with snow and hence it is always recommended to walk carefully over here. As you will move forward you will get a new challenge to pass and you will surely enjoy it. The whole trek journey will let you meet your true self. You will get to know many facts, be it religious, historians, traditional, culture, and many more. You will just leave this place with adifferent version of yourself.

There is nothing hard to reach here. You can definitely be in touch with us and we will solve all your queries. We work for the satisfaction of our customers and provide them the best facilities. Our Bali Pass Trek package is also the most affordable one in the industry and hence adds another comfort level for our customers.

The level of trek is moderate. The tal is located between meadows and beautiful rhododendron flowers. You will also see a beautiful waterfall. Set up your tent and stay overnight amidst beautiful surroundings.

Detail Itinerary

Day 1: Dehradun to Sankri (6,400ft)

Your first day will start at Dehradun Railway Station. You will drive for just about 10 hours to arrive at Sankri. Sankri town lies in the scope of Govind Wildlife Sanctuary, Uttarkashi. In transit from Dehradun to Sankri, you will cross different wonderful landscapes of nature. You will partake in this excursion. You will go over Kempty Waterfalls, Nainbagh, and some more. The town is encircled by a few pinnacles like Swargrohini and more.

In the wake of arriving at Sankri, you will begin feeling the genuine frenzy and energy for the impending journey. In the event that you reach early, you might investigate the town. You will see a few houses comprised of mud, dhabas that serve conventional food, local people in customary outfits, and considerably more. You will remain in the visitor house for the time being.

Day 2: Sankri to Seema via Taluka (6,400ft to 8,500ft via 6,700ft)

After breakfast at the guest house, you will drive to Taluka. Upon reaching Taluka, your trek will begin. The trail follows the Supin River, with many natural sights along the way. You will pass several bridges made of wood and concrete before arriving at an open ground after some distance.

As you continue, you will reach Ghangad Village, where you can experience the warm hospitality of the locals. After crossing a picturesque path with dhabas and water streams, you will reach Osla Village, a well-known tourist spot. From here, it will take around an hour to reach Seema. Along the way, you’ll encounter diverse flora and fauna. At Seema, you will set up camp and rest overnight in tents.

Day 3: Seema to Rainbasera (8,500ft to 10,200ft)

On Day 3, your journey will take you to Rainbasera, following the Supin River. You will first pass Debshu Bugyal, a large meadow perfect for a short rest. As you continue along the steep trail, you’ll get a breathtaking view of Oslo town behind you.

Debshu Bugyal is vast, and you will be able to rest here. From this spot, you can also see the peaks like Kala Nag. The path ahead is quite tricky, so it is important to walk with caution and follow your guide’s instructions. You will face a steep ascent, but eventually, you will reach Rainbasera, your campsite for the day. Set up camp and enjoy your time with your fellow trekkers overnight in tents.

Day 4: Rainbasera to Ruinsara Tal

Day 4 offers a delightful trek, leading you to the stunning Ruinsara Tal. As you walk, you will encounter glaciers and streams, adding to the beauty of the surroundings. The Ruinsara Tal campsite is known for its stunning beauty, surrounded by meadows and snow-capped peaks.

From Ruinsara Tal, you can see magnificent peaks like Bandarpoonch, Kala Nag, and White Peak. The Yamunotri temple is also located nearby, providing a chance to explore and enjoy the breathtaking views of this area.

Day 5: Ruinsara Tal to Odari (11,800ft to 13,100ft)

On Day 5, you’ll embark on a short trek to Odari, which is a 4 km hike through forests and across streams. The path becomes steep, but you’ll soon arrive at a meadow offering spectacular views of the surrounding higher peaks.

Once you reach Odari, which means “Cave,” you will see a natural stone cave. The area offers a chance to explore and rest. Your guide might also give some instructions to help you with future treks. Stay in Odari overnight in tents.

Day 6: Odari to Bali Col Camp (13,100ft to 15,100ft)

On Day 6, the trek from Odari to Bali Col Camp will take you through a steep, difficult path. The climb is challenging, with loose rocks along the way, so caution is key. Be sure to keep your water bottles filled and take proper precautions.

You will be trekking to a high altitude, offering a new adventure and experience. It can get very cold at night, so make sure to carry warm clothes to stay comfortable while camping in tents for the night.

Day 7: Bali Col Camp to Lower Dhamni via Bali Pass

Day 7 is the highlight of your Bali Pass trek, as you reach Lower Dhamni via Bali Pass. Begin the day by fueling up for an exciting trek filled with snow-covered terrain. After about 2-3 hours of hiking, you will reach the top of the pass, where you will be rewarded with panoramic views of Swargarohini, Bandarpoonch, and other Garhwal peaks, along with the Yamunotri Valley on the right.

This is a perfect spot for taking pictures of the breathtaking scenery. After taking in the views, the descent begins, which can be a thrilling experience. However, be cautious while descending as the snow-covered path can be slippery. Follow your guide closely and stay alert.

After some rest, the journey to Lower Dhamni continues. This portion of the trek is simple and straight, passing through beautiful forests. Eventually, you will reach your campsite, where you will camp for the last night of your Bali Pass trek.

Day 8: Lower Dhamni to Jaan Ki Chatti and Drive to Dehradun

Day 8 marks the end of your trek. You will trek from Lower Dhamni to Jaan Ki Chatti, a scenic area with downhill terrain. After reaching the campsite, the final journey takes you back to Dehradun.
